About Anna University
Anna University was established on 4th September, 1978 as a unitary type of University. This University was named after Late Dr. C. N. Annadurai, former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu. It offers higher education in Engineering, Technology, Architecture and Applied Sciences relevant to the current and projected needs of the society. Besides promoting research and disseminating knowledge gained therefrom, it fosters cooperation between the academic and industrial communities. The University was formed by bringing together and integrating four well known technical institutions in the city of Madras (now Chennai) namely:
College of Engineering (CEG) (1794)
Alagappa College of Technology (ACT) (1944)
Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) (1949)
School of Architecture & Planning (SAP) (1957)

ABOUT MADRAS INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY

In 1949, Shri. C. Rajam, gave the newly independent India-Madras Institute of Technology, so that MIT could establish the strong technical base it needed to take its place in the world. It was the rare genius and daring of its founder that made MIT offer courses like Aeronautical Engineering, Automobile Engineering, Electronics Engineering and Instrument Technology for the first time in our country.

Now it also provides technical education in other engineering fields such as Production Technology, Information Technology, Computer Technology and Rubber and Plastic Technology. It was merged with Anna University in the year 1978.  MIT has produced great scientist like Dr.A.P.J.Abdul Kalam, ISRO Chairman K.Sivan, versatile genius like Sujatha, Ramesh Praba and many more. The broad-based education, coupled with practice-oriented training in their specialty, has enabled the students of MIT to handle with skill and success a wide variety of technical problems. MIT has developed into an important centre of engineering education and earned an excellent reputation both in India and abroad.

ABOUT THE DEPARTMENT
The Department of Production Technology, MIT Campus, Anna University, proudly celebrates its Golden Jubilee (1977û2027), marking 50 Years of Excellence in engineering education, research, and innovation. Established in 1977 as the fifth department of MIT Campus, it has grown into a leading multidisciplinary centre for Manufacturing, Automation, Robotics, and Mechatronics, consistently aligning academic learning with modern industrial and technological needs. The department offers a strong portfolio of programmes, including B.E. Production Engineering, B.E. Robotics and Automation, M.E. Manufacturing Engineering, and M.E. Mechatronics, along with part-time B.E. Mechanical Engineering and Ph.D. research opportunities. A notable milestone in its journey was the introduction of one of IndiaÆs earliest full-time M.E. Mechatronics programmes in 1999, reflecting its leadership in interdisciplinary and technology-driven education. Over the years, the department has contributed significantly to academic excellence, impactful research, and industry engagement. It has hosted and supported major conferences and programmes such as AIRPAM, ADFORM, RIPE, and RETNAM, along with seminars, FDPs, and technical events. Supported by agencies such as AICTE, DST-FIST, UGC-DRS/SAP, DRDO (NRB), DST-SERB, CMRG, and TANII, and strengthened by NBA accreditation and UGC-UPE recognition, the department continues to nurture future-ready engineers for Industry 5.0 and beyond.
